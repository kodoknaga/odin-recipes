Panada

Fried bread with fish filling from Manado

Serving size : 20pcs
Original source : Uli's Kitchen Cookpad

Crispy deep fried bread with juicy cakalang fish might be a good choice to fill your tummy. Find out the secrets of the taste and recreate it in your beloved kitchen

Ingredients

  • Bread dough

    • 300 grams high-protein flour
    • 45 grams white sugar
    • 1 teaspoon of yeast
    • 1 Egg
    • 225ml of warm coconut milk (santan)
    • 1/2 tablespoon of butter
    • Salt as needed
    • Cooking/vegetable oil as needed to grease a bowl

  • Filling

    • 200 grams of steamed & shredded tuna/cakalang fish
    • A handful of basil
    • 1 stem of sliced ​scallions
    • 1 stem of lemongrass (thinly sliced the white part)
    • Leftover coconut milk from the bread dough
    • Salt as needed
    • Sugar as needed
    • Powdered stock (Optional)
    • 1 pandan leaf
    • 2 lime leaves

  • Seasoning

    • 3 cloves of garlic
    • 4 cloves of shallots
    • 3 long red chilli
    • 20 bird's eye chili (adjust to your liking)
    • 2 tablespoon of cooking oil

  • Frying

    • Cooking oil

How to make

  • The bread :

    1. Mix flour, sugar, and yeast with a spatula
    2. Whipped the egg, then add it into the mix
    3. Knead the dough with your hand
    4. Add the warm coconut milk slowly until the dough is smooth and elastic. Keep kneading to mix all the ingredients
    5. Add salt and butter to the mix, then keep kneading for approximately 15 minutes
    6. Grease a big bowl with oil to let the dough rest and rise
    7. Put the rounded dough in an oil greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, then let it rest for an hour

  • The filling :

    1. Pound all the seasoning ingredients until smooth
    2. Sauté the seasoning, then add pandan leaf and lime leaves. Keep sauté until fragrance
    3. Add fish, basil, scallions, sliced lemongrass, and leftover coconut milk. Cook until shriveled
    4. Add salt, sugar, and powdered stock into the mix. Cook until dried up, then take it off the stove and let it cool down

  • Wrapping and frying :

    1. Take the dough after it rises then punch and knead it
    2. Separate the dough into approx. 20 parts (30gr each)
    3. Flatten out the dough, add the filling, then wrap the filling and shape it into pastel-like shape (see below)
    4. Heat up the cooking oil on medium fire
    5. Fried the panada until golden brown, take it off the fire, strain it with tissue, then serve while it's hot
